The Outsiders

A novel by S.E. Hinton

About the Characters

  • Ponyboy Curtis:
    • Youngest member of the Greasers.
    • Intelligent and sensitive.
    • Narrator of the story.
  • Johnny Cade:
    • Timid and abused by his parents.
    • Close friend of Ponyboy.
    • Finds refuge and friendship in the gang.
  • Dallas Winston (Dally):
    • Tough and reckless.
    • Protective of his friends.
    • Has a troubled past.
  • Sodapop Curtis:
    • Ponyboy's handsome and charming older brother.
    • Works at a gas station.
    • Struggles with family conflicts.
  • Darry Curtis:
    • Ponyboy and Sodapop's oldest brother.
    • Responsible and hardworking.
    • Feels burdened by his parental role.

About the Novel

"The Outsiders" is a coming-of-age novel set in the 1960s, written by S.E. Hinton. It follows the story of Ponyboy Curtis, a Greaser, and his gang as they navigate the challenges of growing up in a divided society. The Greasers, like Ponyboy, Johnny, and Dally, are from the wrong side of the tracks, while the Socs, like Bob and Randy, are the rich kids with all the advantages.

About the Author

S.E. Hinton was born on July 22, 1948, in Tulsa, Oklahoma. She wrote "The Outsiders" while still in high school, inspired by her observations of the social divide among her peers. Despite facing criticism for her age and gender, Hinton's novel became a groundbreaking work in young adult literature.

Since its publication, Hinton's novel has become a staple in young adult literature.
